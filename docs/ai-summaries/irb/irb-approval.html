Amendment Application

Summary of Proposed Change

This amendment proposes a modification to data analysis procedures only. The study will incorporate an AI-based analytic method to process existing gameplay log data and generate teacher-facing narrative summaries of student performance.

There are no changes to:

  • Study population
  • Recruitment procedures
  • Consent process or consent documents
  • Participant interaction or intervention
  • Data collection methods or instruments

Description of the Modification

The current study collects gameplay log data reflecting student interactions within the MHS game. These logs include behavioral and performance events such as:

  • Number of attempts to complete tasks
  • Choices made within the game
  • Progression through levels or activities

Under this amendment, these existing, de-identified data will be processed using an AI-based analytic system to generate short summaries for teachers. These summaries may include:

  • Indicators of student progress
  • Identification of common errors or challenges
  • General instructional suggestions

The AI system functions as an analytic tool, replacing or augmenting traditional rule-based or statistical summaries.

Data Description

  • Data type: Gameplay interaction/performance logs
  • Examples: Attempts, choices, progression events
  • Identifiability: Fully de-identified; no PII collected or transmitted
  • Source: Existing data already approved under the current protocol
  • No new variables or sensitive data elements are introduced

Data Use and Flow

  • Data is transmitted to a controlled AI processing environment
  • Processing generates teacher-facing summaries only
  • No raw data is shared outside authorized MHS systems
  • No external/public data sharing occurs
  • Outputs are limited to instructional support summaries

Privacy and Confidentiality

  • No personally identifiable information (PII) is used
  • Data remains de-identified throughout processing
  • Access restricted to authorized project personnel
  • Security and storage practices remain unchanged from the approved protocol

Risk Assessment Minimal Risk

This modification does not increase risk to participants.

  • No change in participant experience
  • No new data collection
  • No introduction of identifiable or sensitive data
  • No change in data exposure or sharing

The activity remains minimal risk, consistent with the currently approved study.

Impact on Participants

None. Participants will not be aware of or affected by this change, as it only alters internal data analysis and reporting methods.

Consent Process

No changes. Existing consent materials remain accurate, as:

  • No new data is collected
  • No new uses of identifiable data are introduced
  • The nature of participation is unchanged

Justification / Purpose of Change

The purpose of this modification is to improve the usefulness and clarity of teacher-facing feedback by leveraging AI to generate more flexible and informative summaries of student gameplay performance.

This is an exploratory enhancement to:

  • Evaluate the quality and usefulness of AI-generated feedback
  • Gather teacher input on instructional value
  • Assess feasibility in terms of time and cost

AI Use Justification for MHS Project

Overview

This project proposes the use of an AI-based analytic tool to process existing, de-identified gameplay performance data in order to generate teacher-facing summaries that support instructional decision-making.

The AI system does not introduce new data collection, new participant interaction, or changes to the study design. It functions solely as an enhancement to existing data analysis methods.

Nature of the Data

The data used by the AI system consists exclusively of de-identified gameplay logs, including:

  • Task attempts (e.g., number of tries)
  • In-game choices (e.g., selection paths)
  • Progression through activities or levels

No personally identifiable information (PII) is included in the dataset. The data represents student interaction with the game system, not student identity.

Purpose of AI Use

The AI system is used to:

  • Summarize patterns in student performance
  • Identify common errors or challenges
  • Provide general instructional suggestions to teachers

This replaces or augments traditional rule-based or statistical summaries currently used in the system.

Role of AI in the Study

The AI system:

  • Does not make decisions about participants
  • Does not alter gameplay or student experience
  • Does not impact grading or evaluation directly

It generates advisory, teacher-facing summaries only, which educators may use at their discretion.

Data Handling and Security

  • All data remains within a secure, access-controlled MHS environment
  • No data is shared publicly or with unauthorized third parties
  • No identifiable data is introduced at any stage
  • Data storage and security practices remain unchanged from the approved protocol

Risk Assessment Minimal Risk

This use of AI presents no greater than minimal risk because:

  • No new data is collected
  • No identifiable or sensitive data is used
  • No change occurs in participant interaction
  • Data exposure and handling remain unchanged

The AI system functions as a data processing tool, not a new research intervention.

Human Oversight

  • Teachers receive training on appropriate interpretation of AI-generated feedback
  • AI outputs are not treated as authoritative or automated decisions
  • Human judgment remains central to instructional use

Exploratory Nature

This work is exploratory and intended to:

  • Evaluate the usefulness of AI-generated feedback
  • Understand how teachers interpret and apply summaries
  • Assess feasibility in terms of time, cost, and instructional value
Conclusion: The proposed use of AI represents a change in analytic methodology only, without altering the scope of human subjects involvement, data sensitivity, or risk profile of the study.

AI Risk Clarification & Mitigation Statement

AI Output Reliability

AI-generated summaries are non-deterministic and advisory in nature, not treated as ground truth or formal assessment.

Mitigation

  • Outputs are presented as suggestive insights, not conclusions
  • Teachers are explicitly instructed to apply professional judgment

Bias and Interpretation

The AI system may reflect patterns in the input data but does not profile students, assign labels or categories to individuals, or make evaluative judgments about student ability.

Mitigation

  • Outputs are limited to task-level and interaction-level observations
  • No demographic or personal attributes are included or inferred

Hallucination / Incorrect Output

AI systems may generate incorrect or imprecise summaries.

Mitigation

  • Outputs are constrained to provided gameplay logs and defined game context
  • Teachers are trained to treat outputs as supporting information only

Data Leakage / Privacy Concerns

Risk: AI systems exposing sensitive data.

Mitigation

  • Only de-identified data is used
  • Processing occurs in a controlled environment
  • No prompts include identifiable or sensitive content
  • No external/public model training on this data

Automation Risk

Risk: AI replacing human decision-making.

Mitigation

  • AI does not automate decisions
  • No grading, placement, or evaluation actions are taken by AI
  • Human educators remain fully in control

Scope Limitation

The AI system is strictly limited to processing gameplay logs and generating teacher-facing summaries. It does not interact with students, modify gameplay, or influence participant behavior.

Regulatory Positioning: This use of AI is best understood as an advanced analytic tool applied to de-identified data, equivalent in risk profile to existing statistical or rule-based methods.
"The AI does not introduce new data, new risk, or new interaction — it changes only how already-approved data is summarized."
